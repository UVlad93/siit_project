Maximum Strength exercises are exercises performed at 1 repetition max (1RM) and they are meant to test the individual's capacity of exerting high intensity effort for a short period of time. In order for the testing session to be relevant, you have to be fully rested (at least 48-72 hours from the last session) and warmed up to avoid the risk of injury.

List of exercises:

  1. Max Hangs on 20mm: This exercise tests the individual's capacity of performing a half crimp deadhang on 20mm holds for 7 seconds with maximum added weight.
  2. One Arm Hangs on 20mm: This exercises tests the individual's capacity of performing a one arm half crimp hang on 20mm holds for 5 seconds with maximum added weight. If you are not able to do it at bodyweight, use a pulley system to substract weight. Warning! This exercise is for climbers who have a solid training history and are not injured!
  3. Front Lever Hold: This exercise tests the individual's capacity of performing front lever for the longest time.
  4. Max Added Weight Pull Up: This exercise tests the individual's capacity of performing one pull up with the maximum amount of added weight. Use a harness or belt to connect the weights to your body

Endurance exercises are meant to increase the individual's capacity of exerting low intensity effort for a long time.

List of exercises:

  1. Max Time Hang on 20mm/10mm: This exercise improves the individual's capacity of performing bodyweight deadhangs on 20mm/10mm holds for the longest time possible
  2. Max Timed Bicycle Crunches: This exercise increases the individual's capacity of performing bicycle crunches for the longest time possible
  3. Max Hollow Body Hold: this exercise tests the individual's capacity of performing a hollow body hold on the floor for the longest time possible.
